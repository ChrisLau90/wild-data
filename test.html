River Adur Restoration

by Chris | Aug 28, 2023 | Features

Most rivers and their tributaries in southern England have been straightened, dredged, canalised or engineered in some way. The aim is to produce a fast-flowing conduit which removes water from adjacent fields quickly and efficiently via ditches and underground land drains in order to ‘improve’ land for agricultural production, be it arable or pasture.

A report on the likelihood of water vole (Arvicola amphibius) presence at the Knepp Estate 2016

by Chris | Sep 14, 2023 | Surveys & Reports

The survey method for water voles entails searching amongst suitable waterside vegetation for feeding stations and latrines, with the optimum survey season being between April and September when water voles are most active.

Knepp River Restoration Dragonfly Survey

by Chris | Sep 13, 2023 | Surveys & Reports

This set of surveys, starting in 2015, were a follow-on survey from the dragonfly surveys carried out in the summer of 2005 by Paul James. The original survey method has been enhanced since 2016 to provide more consistent and comparable recording going forward which will better show the changes as the river restoration settles in and vegetation becomes more established.

2015/2016 Repeat of the Baseline Moth Survey

by Chris | Apr 14, 2020 | Surveys & Reports

The purpose of the survey was to repeat the baseline moth survey that was carried out by Dr Tim Freed in 2005, replicating as best as possible the survey dates and trap locations to make the surveys consistent and comparable. Surveyors contributing to these surveys were Dave Green, Tony Davis and the author.
